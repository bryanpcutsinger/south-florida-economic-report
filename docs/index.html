Employment & Wage Trends

Between 2019 and 2025, employment in Palm Beach went from 620,166 to 659,535, an increase of 6.3%. Average annual wages went from $58,240 to $76,180, rising 30.8% over the same period.

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Industry Composition

In 2025 Q3, Palm Beach County's private sector employed 595,668 workers across 20 industries. The largest by employment share were Health Care & Social Assistance (17.2%), Retail Trade (12.8%), and Accommodation & Food Services (11.6%).

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Wage Landscape

In 2025 Q3, the highest-paying industry in Palm Beach was Management of Companies ($189,436/year), while the lowest was Accommodation & Food Services ($35,256/year). The overall average was $75,642.

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Industry Growth

In 2025 Q3, the fastest-growing industry was Mining (+4.9% YoY), while Real Estate saw the largest decline (-11.1%). Overall, 10 of 19 industries expanded while 9 contracted. The largest wage increase was in Manufacturing (+17.6%), while Wholesale Trade saw wages fall (-5.6%).

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Wage–Employment Landscape

In 2025 Q3, each dot represents a 2-digit NAICS sector. Dot size reflects the number of establishments. Health Care & Social Assistance and Retail Trade employ large workforces at below-average wages.

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Establishment Dynamics

In 2025 Q3, Palm Beach County's private sector saw a net gain of 2,515 establishments year-over-year. 17 industries added firms while 1 lost them. Professional & Technical Services led with +588 new establishments. Information saw the largest decline (-8).

Source: BLS QCEW — Quarterly

Location Quotients

A location quotient (LQ) compares an industry's local employment share to the national average. An LQ above 1.0 means the industry is more concentrated in Palm Beach than in the U.S. overall. The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) measures how concentrated employment is across industries. Palm Beach County's private sector HHI is 0.093, indicating a diversified economy. The trend has been stable over the past year.
